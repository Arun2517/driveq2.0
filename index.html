System Overview

What is DriveQ 2.0?

A next-generation V2V communication system designed to enhance road safety, optimize traffic management, and support smart transportation ecosystems.

The Problem

Modern vehicles lack real-time peer-to-peer communication. Drivers have no advance warning of hazards beyond their line of sight, leading to preventable accidents and inefficient traffic flow.

Our Solution

DriveQ 2.0 uses ESP32 hardware with LoRa/DSRC/C-V2X radios and OBD-II vehicle data to create a mesh of communicating vehicles, feeding an AI engine that predicts collisions before they occur.

The Goal

Real-world deployment across fleet, emergency, and public road scenarios — with a fully open-sourced platform and follow-up IEEE publication upon project completion.

Key Features

What DriveQ 2.0 Does

Six core capabilities designed for real-world intelligent transportation scenarios.

Real-time V2V Communication

Sub-50ms peer-to-peer messaging between vehicles using LoRa, DSRC, and C-V2X protocols.

Vehicle Telemetry (OBD-II & CAN)

Live data extraction — speed, RPM, throttle, braking — directly from the vehicle's OBD-II port via CAN Bus.

AI-Based Collision Prediction

Closed Form Neural Networks (CFNN) process telemetry streams to predict collision risk in real time.

Secure & Lightweight Communication

AES encryption with anti-spoofing authentication — built for low-latency, resource-constrained ESP32 hardware.

Emergency Vehicle Prioritization

Automatic right-of-way signalling for ambulances and fire trucks using V2V broadcast.

Traffic Optimization & Intelligence

Aggregate vehicle data feeds a cloud analytics layer to improve routing and reduce congestion.

AI Engine

AI Collision Prediction

Predicting possible collisions before they occur using Closed Form Neural Networks (CFNN).

DriveQ's CFNN model ingests real-time telemetry from OBD-II (speed, brake pressure, acceleration) and fuses it with GPS proximity data from neighbouring vehicles to compute a collision risk score every 100ms.

Unlike conventional neural networks, CFNN offers deterministic computation time — critical for embedded systems where latency guarantees are non-negotiable.

Real Vehicle Deployment Plan

Road to Real-World Testing

A four-phase deployment strategy from controlled lab testing to full public road deployment.

01

Two Vehicle Testing

Controlled V2V link between two ESP32-equipped vehicles in a closed lot.

02

Telemetry Collection

OBD-II data logging at scale to train and validate the CFNN collision model.

03

Closed Environment

Multi-vehicle deployment in a private road — stress testing latency, range, and AI prediction accuracy.

04

Public Road Deployment

Real traffic conditions — fleet vehicles, emergency prioritisation, and live dashboard monitoring.
